# T20 Evolution — Source Analyses for the Two Blog Posts

This document holds the **evidence base** for the two planned posts:

1. **Batting evolution: Gayle → Suryavanshi** — how T20 batting transformed at the macro level and what it tells us about individual players from different eras
2. **Bowling evolution: Malinga → Bumrah + spin sub-arcs** — how bowler types have fared as batting has accelerated

All figures are from the current DB (9,502 matches, 2.13M deliveries) using IPL data unless otherwise noted. Generated by `scripts/smoke_era.py` and the `season_metrics` module. Era-relative WAR is from `t20x.ratings.era_relative`.

**Headline numbers (2008 → 2025):**
- Run rate: **+16%** (8.31 → 9.64)
- Six %: **+65%** (4.8 → 7.9)
- Dot %: **−15%** (38.0 → 32.3)
- Boundary %: **+20%** (18.0 → 21.6)

The 2024–2025 step-change is the biggest in IPL history. **2024 alone added more RPO than the previous 5 years combined.**

### Phase-specific RPO — where the change happened

| Season | Powerplay | Middle | Death |
|-------:|----------:|-------:|------:|
| 2008   | 7.70 | 7.99 | 9.95 |
| 2013   | 6.97 | 7.24 | 9.57 |
| 2018   | 8.42 | 8.06 | 10.14 |

| 2025   | **9.62** | 9.03 | 10.96 |
| 2026   | **10.44** | 9.30 | 10.43 |

**The powerplay revolution is the largest single shift.** Powerplay RPO went from 7.7 (2008) to **9.62 (2025) — +25%**, the biggest of all three phases. Middle overs +13%. Death overs only +10%.

> ### Blog opener insight
>
> By 2025, IPL **powerplay scoring (9.62 RPO)** has nearly caught up with what **death-overs scoring was in 2008 (9.95 RPO)**. The early-overs assault is the defining shift of the modern T20 era.

## II. Player career arcs — the lineage

Era-adjusted per-season WAR for canonical IPL batters (peak season highlighted).

### Chris Gayle — the Universe Boss (total +26.33 WAR)

| Season | WAR | Balls | Per-ball WPA |
|--------|----:|------:|-------------:|
| 2009 (KKR) | **+5.95** | 115 | +0.044 |
| 2011 (RCB debut) | +2.82 | 342 | +0.003 |
| 2013 (the 175* year) | +4.00 | 466 | +0.001 |
| 2018 (KXIP rebirth) | **+5.12** | 261 | +0.013 |
| 2021 (last full season) | +0.32 | 160 | −0.003 |

**Key narrative**: Gayle's **2009 was the most dominant single short-season stretch in IPL history** — +5.95 WAR over just 115 balls — but came in only 2 KKR appearances. His 2018 KXIP renaissance at +5.12 over 261 balls was a "Gayle is back" moment, era-adjusted to Kohli's career peak.

### Virat Kohli — the volume king (total +19.95 WAR)

| Season | WAR | Balls | Per-ball WPA |
|--------|----:|------:|-------------:|
| 2008 (debut, age 19) | −0.50 | 168 | −0.009 |
| 2015 (1st era peak) | **+2.82** | 389 | +0.001 |
| 2016 (the 973-runs season) | +2.41 | 655 | −0.002 |
| 2024 (comeback peak) | +2.31 | 497 | −0.001 |
| 2026 *(partial)* | −0.19 | 75 | +0.000 |

**Key narrative**: Kohli's per-ball WPA is rarely above replacement (he's an anchor, not a power-hitter), but **volume sustains his value**. The 2016 season — 973 runs — produced +2.41 WAR; the 2024 comeback at age 35 produced +2.31. Volume keeps him in the all-time conversation despite never having a Gayle-like dominant short season.

### AB de Villiers — the 360° innovator (total +8.24 WAR)

| Season | WAR | Balls | Per-ball WPA |
|--------|----:|------:|-------------:|
| 2016 (Galaxy phase) | **+2.14** | 415 | −0.001 |
| 2020 | +1.12 | 268 | −0.001 |

**Surprising note**: ABdV's total WAR is *modest* relative to reputation. His SR exceeded peers but volume was limited and per-ball WPA hovered near average. **WPA doesn't reward "highlight reel"; it rewards consistent above-replacement value sustained over balls faced.**

### Brendon McCullum — the originator (total +12.34 WAR)

| Season | WAR | Balls | Per-ball WPA |
|--------|----:|------:|-------------:|
| 2008 (the 158*) | +0.95 | 96 | +0.004 |
| 2009 (peak season) | **+4.88** | 238 | +0.013 |
| 2014 (post-RCB) | +0.32 | 341 | −0.005 |

**The 158\* itself didn't make him +5 WAR.** It made him +0.95 (positive but not dominant). His **+4.88 in 2009 came from sustained quality across 238 balls — the season AFTER the famous innings, not the innings itself.**

### Vaibhav Suryavanshi — the lineage's heir? (2 seasons, total +0.30 WAR)

| Season | WAR | Balls | Per-ball WPA |
|--------|----:|------:|-------------:|
| 2025 (age 13 debut) | −0.10 | 129 | −0.007 |
| 2026 *(partial)* | +0.41 | 78 | **+0.008** |

**Watch this signal**: 2026's +0.008 WPA/ball over 78 balls is **above the replacement bar and on a McCullum-2009-trajectory pace** (McCullum's 2009 peak was +0.013/ball). Suryavanshi is currently producing at a sustained-elite-prospect level for a 13-year-old. The data won't tell us if it lasts, but it tells us the start is real.

## III. The macro bowling evolution (IPL)

### Economy rate by bowler type, by season

| Season | Fast | Med-fast | Leg-break (wrist) | Off-break | LA orthodox | LA wrist (Kuldeep) |
|-------:|-----:|---------:|------------------:|----------:|------------:|-------------------:|
| 2008   | 7.30 | 8.54 | 8.10 | 7.29 | 8.17 | — |
| 2013   | 7.17 | 7.65 | 6.90 | 6.56 | 7.51 | — |
| 2018   | 8.68 | 8.81 | 7.89 | 8.01 | 7.64 | 8.24 |
| 2021   | 8.00 | 8.37 | 7.44 | 7.17 | 7.19 | — |
| 2024   | **9.08** | **9.88** | **9.21** | 7.88 | 7.90 | 8.74 |
| 2025   | **9.36** | **9.63** | **9.36** | **9.13** | 8.77 | 7.40 |
| 2026   | 9.51 | 10.44 | 8.81 | 7.91 | 8.88 | 9.92 |

**Headline numbers (2008 → 2025):**
- Fast bowlers conceded **+28%** more runs/over
- Off-spinners conceded **+25%** more
- Wrist-spin (leg-break) conceded **+16%** more — **best-preserved category**
- Left-arm orthodox conceded **+7%** more — **steadiest**
- Medium-fast (the swing specialists) **+13%**

> ### Blog opener insight
>
> The 2024-2025 batting explosion was **not absorbed equally across bowler types**. Off-spin and pace got destroyed. Left-arm orthodox and wrist-spin held up — by a meaningful margin. The age of generic finger-spin in T20 is over; the era of specialized wrist-spin and disciplined left-arm has arrived.

## V. Working hypotheses for the blog narratives

### Batting post hypotheses
1. **The "Gayle template" is now baseline.** What was revolutionary in 2009 (powerplay assault, high SR) is now standard. The chart of powerplay RPO over time is the central evidence.
2. **Volume vs intensity divide:** Gayle (intensity, low volume) and Kohli (intensity-neutral, high volume) represent the two paths to all-time WAR. ABdV underperforms reputation; McCullum underperforms folklore.
3. **Suryavanshi looks early-McCullum-trajectory** based on 2 partial seasons. Not a prediction; an observation about where his per-ball metrics sit in historical context.

### Bowling post hypotheses
1. **Off-spin is dying in IPL.** From 6.6 RPO peak to 9.1 in 2025. The data is unambiguous.
2. **Wrist spin is the best-preserved discipline.** Even at modern run rates, leg-spinners concede less than fast bowlers per over.
3. **Mystery wrist spinners (Hasaranga, Varun CV) are the next inefficiency** — they're tagged in our DB and will get their own section.
4. **Bumrah is the canonical pace-evolution endpoint** — yorker → variations → death-overs mastery. Need to show his per-phase WAR by season.
